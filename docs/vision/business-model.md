# Business Model

Jasfo operates as a solo broker enablement platform. The business model is straightforward: the platform generates high-confidence commercial real estate leads for the broker, who then executes the leasing transaction and earns a brokerage commission. There is no separate software subscription, no per-lead pricing, and no third-party revenue stream. The platform's value is captured entirely through successful lease transactions.

## Revenue Model

**Primary revenue: brokerage commissions on leases facilitated.** The broker earns a commission on every commercial lease transaction closed. Typical Pune commercial real estate commissions range from 1–2 months of rent as the broker's fee, with the exact percentage varying by property size, location, and negotiation. A single mid-sized lease (5,000–10,000 sq ft) can generate a commission of INR 5–15 lakhs. The platform needs to facilitate only 3–5 leases per year to justify its entire operating cost for a decade.

**The platform is not sold as a product.** There is no SaaS subscription, no white-label offering, and no licensing model. Jasfo is a proprietary tool used exclusively by Jasfo (the broker). This eliminates the overhead of customer support, sales, onboarding, and multi-tenant infrastructure. Every engineering dollar goes into improving lead quality rather than building product features.

**Cost structure is aggressively optimized.** The target monthly operating cost is under $50 (approximately INR 4,000). This covers Make.com automation, Apify scraping credits, AI inference, and hosting. The broker's time investment is 2–3 hours per week: reviewing evidence packages, conducting final qualification calls, and executing outreach. At this cost structure, a single closed deal per year delivers an ROI of approximately 50–100x.

## Unit Economics

**Cost per lead delivered is approximately $1–2.** The full pipeline costs under $50 per week and delivers 20–30 qualified leads. Each lead's fully loaded cost includes scraping, AI inference (multiple model calls), verification, enrichment, and the final Judge evaluation. The cost gate ensures that approximately 80% of spend is concentrated on the 20% of companies that reach the final layers.

**Cost per meeting booked depends on conversion.** If 20–30 leads produce 10–15 meetings per month, the cost per meeting is roughly $3–5. If those meetings produce 2–4 site visits, the cost per site visit is $12–25. These economics improve as the scoring model is calibrated and the broker's conversion process matures.

**The model works because AI costs are declining.** DeepSeek V4 Flash provides approximately 90% of frontier-model capability at approximately 10% of the cost. As AI inference costs continue their historical decline curve, the platform's cost per lead will decrease even as the pipeline processes more signals per company.

## Strategic Positioning

Jasfo occupies a unique position: it has the research depth of an institutional platform with the cost structure of a solo operator. Enterprise CRE tech platforms charge $500–5,000/month for access to their data. Jasfo delivers equivalent or superior intelligence for under $50/month. This cost advantage is structural — the platform was designed from day one around cost optimization rather than bolted on as an afterthought.

The platform does not need to scale to thousands of users to be viable. It needs to facilitate a handful of successful lease transactions per year. This removes the growth pressure that distorts most startup business models and allows the platform to focus on quality rather than velocity.
