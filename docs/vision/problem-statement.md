# Problem Statement

Commercial real estate brokers in Pune face a structural disadvantage against institutional firms. Large brokerages employ dedicated research teams that systematically track market activity, company movements, and space requirements. A solo broker — even an experienced one with deep market knowledge — cannot compete with the research capacity of a five-person team. The result is lost mandates, missed opportunities, and a slow erosion of market share to firms that can afford intelligence at scale.

## Core Pain Points

**Manual research is slow and inconsistent.** A broker researching a single company manually might check the company website, LinkedIn profile, news mentions, and perhaps a database like Zauba or Tofler. This process takes 15–30 minutes per company and produces inconsistent results depending on the broker's energy, focus, and methodology. For a pipeline of 10,000 companies, manual research is simply impossible. The broker is forced to rely on pattern recognition, intuition, and the limited subset of companies they can research in depth.

**Brokers lose leads to institutional competitors.** When a 200-person IT services company in Hinjewadi starts expanding, the institutional firms with research teams will know within days. They monitor job postings, news mentions, regulatory filings, and LinkedIn hiring patterns. The solo broker might discover this company six weeks later when the space requirement is already publicly known and multiple brokers are already pitching. Being second means competing on price rather than value.

**No systematic lead intelligence exists for the Pune market.** Enterprise tools like CoStar, Reonomy, and CompStak serve US markets but are either unavailable or impractical for the Indian commercial real estate market. Indian alternatives are fragmented and focus primarily on residential real estate. The result is a data vacuum that institutional firms fill with in-house research teams and solo brokers fill with guesswork.

**The market is too large for relationship-only coverage.** Pune has over 5,000 active companies across IT parks in Hinjewadi, Kharadi, Baner, Viman Nagar, and the traditional business districts. A solo broker can maintain meaningful relationships with perhaps 100–200 key decision-makers. The remaining 4,800+ companies are invisible to the broker until they actively enter the market — at which point every broker already knows about them.

## Market Impact

The consequences of this intelligence gap are measurable. Brokers lose an estimated 40–60% of potential mandates to competitors who identify the opportunity first. Time from company signal to broker outreach averages 2–3 weeks for solo brokers versus 2–3 days for institutional teams. Outreach quality suffers because brokers lack the context to have informed conversations — they call cold rather than arriving with knowledge of the company's growth trajectory, space needs, and decision-making structure.

Jasfo exists to close this gap. By building an AI-powered intelligence layer that operates continuously and systematically, the platform gives a solo broker the research capacity of an institutional team while maintaining the cost structure of a solo operation.
